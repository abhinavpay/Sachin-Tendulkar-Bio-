Early Life

Tendulkar was born in Dadar, Bombay, at Nirmal Nursing Home in a Maharashtrian family.

He studied at the Indian Education Society’s New English School in Bandra .

At the age of 16, Tendulkar made his first test debut against Pakistan, where he scored 215 runs at an average of 35.8.

At the age of 23, in 1996, Sachin was made the captain of the Indian cricket team.

He became the first player to score 15000 runs in ODI in the second match of the Future Cup against South Africa.

Awards And Achivements

On February 4, 2014, Tendulkar was conferred the Bharat Ratna by the then President of India, the late Pranab Mukherjee.

Sachin Tendulkar got Padma Vibhushan, India's
second-highest civilian honour on 2008.

Sachin Tendulkar was awarded the Padma Shri, India's fourth highest civilian award in 1999.
